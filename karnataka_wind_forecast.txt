1. Karnataka Wind Power — State Overview
Karnataka is India's #1 wind energy state as of FY 2024-25, with a cumulative installed wind capacity of 7,351 MW, having added a record 1,331.48 MW in FY25 alone. The wind resource is concentrated in the northern and central districts — Vijayapura, Gadag, Chitradurga, Koppal, Ballari, Davangere, and Belagavi — where the Deccan Plateau's elevation creates consistent wind flows, especially during the Southwest Monsoon (June–September).

5. Recommended ML Modeling Pipeline
Step 1 — Data Collection & Alignment
Download ERA5/MERRA-2 historical wind data (1980-2024) for Karnataka bounding box (14°N–18°N, 74°E–78°E)
Collect SCADA/SLDC actual generation data for each plant (15-min or 1-hr resolution)
Obtain static parameters: power curves, hub heights, lat/lon, installed capacity per plant from KREDL / developer data
Align all data to a common UTC timestamp index; resample to 1-hour resolution
Step 2 — Preprocessing
Remove curtailed periods (use SCADA curtailment flag or SLDC dispatch instructions)
Filter turbine fault/maintenance periods using status codes
Apply air density correction to wind speed using co-located temperature and pressure
Extrapolate 10m NWP wind to hub height using power law (α) or log law (z₀)
Cyclically encode hour and day-of-year using sin/cos transformation
Step 3 — Feature Engineering
Compute Wind Power Density (WPD = 0.5 × ρ × V³) as a super-feature
Add rolling statistics: 1h, 6h, 24h mean/std of wind speed
Compute NWP forecast error (bias) as a correction feature
Create plant-level embeddings (or one-hot encode plant ID) for multi-plant models
Fit Weibull k and c parameters per month/season per plant location
Step 4 — Model Architecture Options
Baseline: Gradient Boosting (XGBoost/LightGBM) — fast, interpretable, strong for tabular wind data
Time-series: LSTM / Temporal Fusion Transformer (TFT) — captures diurnal and seasonal patterns
Physics-informed: Combine power curve lookup with ML residual correction
Ensemble: Blend NWP-physics model + ML model for best day-ahead accuracy
Step 5 — Evaluation Metrics
RMSE (Root Mean Square Error) — penalizes large errors; primary metric
MAE (Mean Absolute Error) — robust to outliers
MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) — caution: unreliable near zero generation
Normalized RMSE (nRMSE = RMSE/Prated) — compare across plants of different size
Skill Score vs. persistence forecast — measures improvement over naive baseline


6. Karnataka Wind Seasonality — Key Patterns for Model

Karnataka's Tuppadahalli wind farm (Chitradurga) was recognized as India's best-performing wind farm in FY 2021-22, with a capacity factor significantly exceeding the state average. The Gadag and Vijayapura districts consistently show the highest annual mean wind speeds (7-8 m/s at 100m), making them the model's highest-confidence prediction zones.
